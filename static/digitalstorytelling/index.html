Course Description

This EPEC is designed as hybrid workshop-seminar that will introduce students to the basics of writing stories for digital media & of the history of digital storytelling in all its forms. No computer expertise is required.

The readings included in this course are numerous, and none of them is by any means necessary. I will be assigning specific readings to different members of the class, and those assigned with particular readings will lead discussions on their assigned articles. These readings are meant to foment discussion & drive you to seek out new material & create inventive and challenging work.

Note: This syllabus is a living document, and as such it will grow and change according to the needs of the course. I will email the class of major changes in readings, but don’t forget to bookmark this page & check on it often, as it may look slightly different.

Anthologies of Digital Works

  • Electronic Literature Collection Volumes 1 (2006), 2 (2011) & 3 (2016)

    For nearly 10 years, the Electronic Literature Organization has been putting out collections of some of the best & cutting edge

  • Rhizome’s Net Art Anthology

    An indelible resource that tracks the history of various forms of netart, videogame art, and online narrative, from its earliest incarnations to the contemporary moment.

  • Vectors Journal

    A journal that utilizes digital tools to investigate academic topics in extremely interesting ways (like retelling the history of the Parisian Letterists through a comic book, or like archiving the oral histories of female inmates.)

Programs for Creating Hypertexts

  • Twine (Mac/Win)

    A free & open-source program for writing basic hypertexts & interactive fiction. The wiki for Twine is also extremely helpful as it contains full documentation & various troubleshooting tips.

  • Godot (Mac/Win)

    A free & open-source engine for creating 2D and 3D games.

  • Cyberduck (Mac/Win)

    A powerful, yet simple-to-use SFTP program for interacting with servers & remote storage. I use this most often when uploading files to my student server (every Hampshire student has one!)

  • Inform (Mac/Win/Linux)

  • Quest (Web/Win)

  • ChoiceScript (Web)

  • inklewriter (Web)

  • philome.la (Web)

  • TADS (Win/Mac/Linux)
